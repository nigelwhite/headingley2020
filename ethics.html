Ethics

headingley.org does not take a neutral position. headingley.org is for those discussions which are community-strengthening. It is not for general discussions.

All sorts of supportive and positive messages have been exchanged since 2004. This is possible when residents (especially new residents) feel comfortable reaching out to others, in the knowledge that they will be received kindly, with respect and good will. headingley.org is here as a platform to help that happen.

headingley.org's aim is to increase community resilience. When residents interact and support each other, in daily life, we discover interconnections within our community. This has all sorts of benefits. These benefits come about informally, spontaneously, maybe off-list, as we find new ways of supporting each other, which fit the needs of the moment. Supportive exchanges in our home community enhance our lives and bring us happiness.

So headingley.org is for community-strengthening discussions rather than general discussions. It is here as a platform for times when we want to create, enhance and strengthen community interconnections. This means choosing not to air some things, not because you don't think them, but because they may set everyone at loggerheads. headingley.org is not here for general discussion of this or that. It is here for, "how can we help each other with this or that?"

Sometimes it is hard to judge..... there may be grey areas..... one person's rant could be another person's reasonable statement. Hopefully, if you keep these stated aims in your mind as you write, everyone will feel at ease.
