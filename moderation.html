Moderation

Such is the level of residents' respect for each other that deliberate moderation has rarely been necessary. We guess this is because of our shared understanding that building community is more important than 'winning' debates. Please see our ethics for more on this.

However, moderation is available to us, as a community. Starting with light-touch approaches and escalating with seriousness, these are......

1. Moderation by our members. If people don't appreciate the tone of a discussion members expresses their dissaproval in email messages on the network itself.

2. Personal requests to cool it, in a private email to an individual member.

3. A 'quiet word' from the admin team, or from Headingley Network, reminding of our ethics.

Since 2004 there has only been one occasion when stronger measures were needed. Again there are a range of strategies available to the admin team, in consultation with respected community members -

  • every email by a member can be held till manually approved
  • a member can have their membership temporarily or peremanently removed
